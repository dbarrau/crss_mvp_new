## Question 2

Under the EU AI Act, what are the mandatory verification steps a hospital (as a 'Deployer') must complete before putting a high-risk AI medical device into service?

### Answer

Under the EU AI Act, a hospital acting as a **deployer** of a **high-risk AI medical device** must complete several mandatory verification and compliance steps before putting the system into service. These steps are derived from the obligations imposed on deployers of high-risk AI systems, as well as the interplay between the AI Act and the MDR 2017/745, since the device is also a medical device.

Below is a structured summary of the **mandatory verification steps**, grounded in the provided regulatory context:

---

### 1. **Confirm the AI System is Classified as High-Risk**
The hospital must verify that the AI system is **legally classified as high-risk** under the AI Act.

- **Legal basis**: [Article 6(1) and (2) EU AI Act](https://example.com) (from [13] Chapter III)
  > "AI systems referred to in Annex III shall be considered to be high-risk."
  > "In addition to the high-risk AI systems referred to in paragraph 1, AI systems referred to in Annex III shall be considered to be high-risk."

- The AI system must either:
  - Be a **safety component of a product** (e.g., a medical device) **covered by Union harmonisation legislation listed in Annex I** (which includes the MDR 2017/745), **and** the product is subject to a **third-party conformity assessment** (e.g., Class IIa, IIb, or III under MDR); **or**
  - Fall under one of the **use cases listed in Annex III** (e.g., biometrics, critical infrastructure, or medical devices used for diagnostic or therapeutic decision-making).

- **For AI medical devices**, the MDR classification (e.g., Class IIa, IIb, III) and intended use determine whether the AI component triggers high-risk status under the AI Act.

- **Verification step**: The hospital must confirm that the AI system is **explicitly listed in Annex III** or is a **safety component of a medical device subject to third-party conformity assessment under MDR**.

---

### 2. **Verify the AI System Has Undergone Conformity Assessment and Bears CE Marking**
The hospital must ensure the AI system (or the medical device containing it) has undergone the **applicable conformity assessment procedure** and bears the **CE marking**.

- **Legal basis**: [Article 8(2) EU AI Act](https://example.com) (from [1] Article 8)
  > "Where a product contains an AI system, to which the requirements of this Regulation as well as requirements of the Union harmonisation legislation listed in Section A of Annex I apply, providers shall be responsible for ensuring that their product is fully compliant with all applicable requirements under applicable Union harmonisation legislation."

- Since the AI system is embedded in a **medical device**, the **MDR conformity assessment** (e.g., via a notified body for Class IIa/IIb/III) applies. The AI Act **does not replace** the MDR conformity assessment but **integrates** with it.

- **Verification step**: The hospital must confirm:
  - The device has a **valid CE marking** under MDR.
  - The **EU declaration of conformity** is available and includes compliance with both MDR and AI Act requirements.
  - For Class IIa/IIb/III devices, a **notified body certificate** under MDR is in place.

---

### 3. **Check the Availability of Required Documentation**
The hospital must verify that the **provider** (manufacturer) has made available the following documentation:

#### a. **Technical Documentation (AI Act)**
- **Legal basis**: [Article 11(1) EU AI Act](https://example.com) (from [13] Chapter III)
  > "The technical documentation of a high-risk AI system shall be drawn up before that system is placed on the market or put into service and shall be kept up-to-date."

- The documentation must include:
  - A **general description** of the AI system (intended purpose, version, interaction with hardware/software).
  - **Risk management system** (Article 9).
  - **Data governance** (Article 10).
  - **Accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity** measures (Article 15).
  - **Human oversight** mechanisms (Article 14).
  - **Automatically generated logs** (Article 12).
  - **Instructions for use** (Article 13).

- **Verification step**: The hospital must request and review the **technical documentation** from the provider to confirm compliance with **Section 2 of Chapter III (AI Act)**.

#### b. **Instructions for Use (AI Act and MDR)**
- **Legal basis**: [Article 13(1) EU AI Act](https://example.com) (from [3] Section 2)
  > "High-risk AI systems shall be accompanied by instructions for use in an official Union language or languages determined by the Member State concerned, which shall be clear, complete, correct and provided in a manner that is understandable to the deployers."

- The instructions must include:
  - The **intended purpose** of the AI system.
  - **Performance characteristics** (e.g., accuracy, limitations).
  - **Risks** and **residual risks**.
  - **Human oversight** requirements.
  - **Operating instructions** and **integration guidance**.

- **Verification step**: The hospital must confirm that the **instructions for use** are provided in a **language understood by its staff** and include all required elements.

#### c. **MDR-Specific Documentation**
- The hospital must also verify the availability of:
  - **EU declaration of conformity** (MDR Article 19).
  - **Summary of safety and clinical performance** (for implantable and Class III devices, MDR Article 32).
  - **Clinical evaluation report** (MDR Annex XIV).
  - **Post-market surveillance plan** (MDR Article 84).

---

### 4. **Verify Compliance with Human Oversight Requirements**
The hospital must ensure the AI system is designed to allow **effective human oversight** during use.

- **Legal basis**: [Article 14(1) EU AI Act](https://example.com) (from [3] Section 2)
  > "High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed in such a way, including with appropriate human-machine interface tools, that they can be effectively overseen by natural persons during the period in which they are in use."

- The hospital must confirm:
  - The AI system includes **tools for human oversight** (e.g., dashboards, alerts, override functions).
  - **Staff are trained** to use these tools and understand the AI system’s outputs.
  - The system does not **automate decision-making without human review** where required (e.g., for life-critical decisions).

- **Verification step**: The hospital must assess whether the **human oversight mechanisms** are **technically feasible** and **operationally implementable** in its workflow.

---

### 5. **Conduct a Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (FRIA)**
The hospital must perform a **Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment** before deploying the AI system.

- **Legal basis**: [Article 27(1) EU AI Act](https://example.com) (from [13] Chapter III)
  > "Deployers of high-risk AI systems shall carry out an assessment of the impact on fundamental rights that the use of such a system may produce, including by drawing up a fundamental rights impact assessment."

- The FRIA must include:
  - Identification of **potential impacts on fundamental rights** (e.g., privacy, non-discrimination, dignity).
  - Assessment of **risks** and **mitigation measures**.
  - Consultation with **relevant stakeholders** (e.g., patients, staff, ethics committees).

- **Verification step**: The hospital must **document the FRIA** and ensure it is **proportionate to the risks** posed by the AI system.

---

### 6. **Ensure Data Protection Compliance**
The hospital must verify that the AI system complies with **GDPR and other data protection laws**.

- **Legal basis**: [Article 10(5) EU AI Act](https://example.com) (from [3] Section 2)
  > "Training, validation and testing data sets shall be subject to data governance and management practices appropriate for the intended purpose of the high-risk AI system."

- The hospital must confirm:
  - The AI system processes **personal data lawfully** (e.g., with patient consent or another legal basis).
  - **Data minimisation** and **purpose limitation** principles are respected.
  - **Technical and organisational measures** are in place to protect data (e.g., encryption, access controls).

- **Verification step**: The hospital must conduct a **Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)** under GDPR if the AI system processes **sensitive health data**.

---

### 7. **Verify Post-Market Monitoring and Reporting Obligations**
The hospital must ensure it can **monitor the AI system’s performance** and **report incidents** during use.

- **Legal basis**: [Article 26(1) EU AI Act](https://example.com) (from [13] Chapter III)
  > "Deployers of high-risk AI systems shall monitor the operation of the high-risk AI system on the basis of the instructions of use."

- The hospital must:
  - **Monitor the AI system’s performance** (e.g., accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity).
  - **Report serious incidents** to the provider and, where applicable, the **national competent authority** (e.g., via the MDR vigilance system).
  - **Keep records** of the AI system’s operation and any incidents.

- **Verification step**: The hospital must establish **internal processes** for monitoring and reporting, aligned with both **AI Act and MDR requirements**.

---

### 8. **Confirm Compliance with MDR-Specific Obligations**
Since the AI system is part of a **medical device**, the hospital must also verify compliance with **MDR obligations**, including:

#### a. **UDI Registration**
- **Legal basis**: [Article 29 MDR](https://example.com) (from [14] Chapter III)
  > "Before placing a device, other than a custom-made device, on the market, the manufacturer shall assign a Basic UDI-DI as defined in Part C of Annex VI to the device and shall provide it to the UDI database."

- **Verification step**: The hospital must confirm the device is **registered in Eudamed** and has a **valid UDI**.

#### b. **Traceability**
- **Legal basis**: [Article 25 MDR](https://example.com) (from [14] Chapter III)
  > "Economic operators shall be able to identify the following to the competent authority... any economic operator to whom they have directly supplied a device."

- **Verification step**: The hospital must ensure it can **trace the device** and its components (including the AI system) through the supply chain.

#### c. **Clinical Evaluation**
- **Legal basis**: [Article 61 MDR](https://example.com) (from [6] Chapter II)
  > "Confirmation of conformity with relevant general safety and performance requirements... shall be based on clinical data providing sufficient clinical evidence."

- **Verification step**: The hospital must confirm the device has undergone **clinical evaluation** and that the **clinical data** support its **intended use**.

### Key Takeaways
- The hospital must **verify compliance with both the AI Act and MDR**, as the AI system is embedded in a medical device.
- The **AI Act imposes additional obligations** (e.g., FRIA, human oversight, post-market monitoring) that go beyond MDR requirements.
- The hospital is **not responsible for conformity assessment** (this is the provider’s obligation), but it must **verify that the provider has fulfilled its obligations**.
- The hospital must **document all verification steps** and **maintain records** to demonstrate compliance.

## Question 3

How do the obligations for High-Risk AI systems under the AI Act overlap with existing MDR requirements, and which 'AI-specific' gaps should a hospital check that aren't covered by a standard CE mark?

### Answer

### **Overlap and Integration of High-Risk AI System Obligations under the AI Act with MDR Requirements**

The **EU AI Act** and **MDR 2017/745** are designed to work together under the **New Legislative Framework (NLF)**. The AI Act explicitly recognises the MDR as a **Union harmonisation legislation** (Annex I, Section A, point 11), meaning that if a **medical device incorporates a high-risk AI system**, the manufacturer must comply with **both regulations simultaneously**. The AI Act provides flexibility to integrate compliance processes to avoid duplication, but it also introduces **AI-specific requirements** that go beyond standard MDR obligations.

---

### **1. Overlapping Obligations (MDR and AI Act)**

The following obligations apply **both** under the MDR and the AI Act, allowing for **integrated compliance**:

#### **(a) Risk Management System**
- **MDR (Annex I, Section 3)**:
  - Manufacturers must establish, implement, document, and maintain a **risk management system** throughout the device lifecycle.
  - The system must identify, analyse, evaluate, and control risks, including those arising from **use error** and **reasonably foreseeable misuse**.
  - Residual risks must be acceptable when weighed against benefits.

- **AI Act (Article 9)**:
  - High-risk AI systems must have a **continuous iterative risk management system** covering the entire lifecycle.
  - The system must identify and analyse **known and reasonably foreseeable risks** to **health, safety, or fundamental rights** (not just patient safety, as in MDR).
  - The AI Act explicitly requires **evaluation of risks under conditions of reasonably foreseeable misuse** (similar to MDR but broader in scope).

**Integration**:
- The **MDR risk management system** can be **expanded** to include **AI-specific risks** (e.g., bias, lack of transparency, adversarial attacks).
- The **AI Act’s risk management system** (Article 9) can be **embedded** into the MDR’s existing risk management framework (Annex I, Section 3).

**Key Quote (AI Act, Article 9(2))**:
> *"The risk management system shall be understood as a continuous iterative process planned and run throughout the entire lifecycle of a high-risk AI system, requiring regular systematic review and updating."*

**Key Quote (MDR, Annex I, Section 3)**:
> *"Risk management shall be understood as a continuous iterative process throughout the entire lifecycle of a device, requiring regular systematic updating."*

---

#### **(b) Technical Documentation**
- **MDR (Annex II)**:
  - Requires **technical documentation** covering:
    - Device description, intended purpose, risk class, and justification.
    - Design and manufacturing information.
    - Benefit-risk analysis and risk management.
    - Clinical evaluation and performance data.
    - Labelling and instructions for use.

- **AI Act (Article 11, Annex IV)**:
  - Requires **AI-specific technical documentation**, including:
    - General description of the AI system (intended purpose, interaction with hardware/software, versions).
    - Data governance (training, validation, testing datasets).
    - Detailed description of the **AI model** (architecture, algorithms, hyperparameters).
    - **Explainability and transparency** measures (e.g., how decisions are made, limitations).
    - **Accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity** measures.
    - **Human oversight** mechanisms.
    - **Post-market monitoring plan** (Article 72).

**Integration**:
- The **MDR technical documentation** (Annex II) can be **supplemented** with **AI-specific details** (e.g., data governance, model explainability, cybersecurity).
- The **AI Act’s technical documentation** (Annex IV) can **reference** the MDR’s existing documentation where applicable (e.g., clinical evaluation, risk management).

**Key Quote (AI Act, Article 11(1))**:
> *"The technical documentation of a high-risk AI system shall be drawn up before that system is placed on the market or put into service and shall be kept up-to date. [...] It shall contain, at a minimum, the elements set out in Annex IV."*

**Key Quote (MDR, Annex II)**:
> *"The technical documentation [...] shall include in particular the elements listed in this Annex."*

---

#### **(c) Post-Market Surveillance (PMS) and Vigilance**
- **MDR (Articles 83–92, Annex III)**:
  - Requires **post-market surveillance (PMS)** to **actively and systematically** collect and review data on device performance and safety.
  - Requires **vigilance reporting** of **serious incidents** and **field safety corrective actions (FSCAs)**.
  - **Periodic Safety Update Reports (PSURs)** for Class IIa, IIb, and III devices.

- **AI Act (Article 72, Chapter IX)**:
  - Requires a **post-market monitoring system** to **actively and systematically** collect, document, and analyse data on AI system performance **throughout its lifetime**.
  - Must include **analysis of interactions with other AI systems** (if relevant).
  - Must allow the provider to **evaluate continuous compliance** with AI Act requirements (e.g., accuracy, robustness, fundamental rights impact).

**Integration**:
- The **MDR PMS system** can be **expanded** to include **AI-specific performance metrics** (e.g., model drift, bias detection, cybersecurity incidents).
- The **AI Act’s post-market monitoring plan** can be **integrated** into the MDR’s **PMS plan** (Annex III) and **vigilance reporting** (Article 87).

**Key Quote (AI Act, Article 72(2))**:
> *"The post-market monitoring system shall actively and systematically collect, document and analyse relevant data which may be provided by deployers or which may be collected through other sources on the performance of high-risk AI systems throughout their lifetime, and which allow the provider to evaluate the continuous compliance of AI systems with the requirements set out in Chapter III, Section 2."*

**Key Quote (MDR, Article 83(1))**:
> *"Manufacturers shall plan, establish, document, implement, maintain and update a post-market surveillance system in a manner that is proportionate to the risk class and appropriate for the type of device."*

---

#### **(d) Conformity Assessment and Notified Bodies**
- **MDR (Article 52, Annexes IX–XI)**:
  - Requires **third-party conformity assessment** for **Class IIa, IIb, and III devices** (and some Class I devices).
  - Notified bodies assess compliance with **general safety and performance requirements (GSPR, Annex I)**.

- **AI Act (Article 43, Annex VI)**:
  - If the **AI system is a safety component of a product** (e.g., a medical device) that requires **third-party conformity assessment under MDR**, the **AI Act’s requirements are assessed as part of the MDR conformity assessment**.
  - The **AI Act does not require a separate conformity assessment** if the MDR already covers it.
  - The **notified body** assessing the medical device must also **verify compliance with AI Act requirements** (e.g., risk management, data governance, transparency).

**Integration**:
- The **MDR conformity assessment** (e.g., Annex IX for Class III devices) will **automatically include AI Act requirements** if the device incorporates a high-risk AI system.
- The **notified body** must have **competence in AI** to assess compliance with **AI-specific requirements** (e.g., data governance, explainability, robustness).

**Key Quote (AI Act, Article 43(3))**:
> *"Where the conformity assessment procedure of the product referred to in paragraph 1 of this Article requires the involvement of a notified body pursuant to the applicable Union harmonisation legislation, the conformity assessment of the high-risk AI system shall be carried out as part of that procedure. The notified body shall verify the compliance of the high-risk AI system with the requirements set out in Section 2 of this Chapter."*

---

#### **(e) Quality Management System (QMS)**
- **MDR (Article 10(9), Annex IX)**:
  - Requires manufacturers to establish a **quality management system (QMS)** covering:
    - Regulatory compliance.
    - Risk management.
    - Design and manufacturing controls.
    - Post-market surveillance.

- **AI Act (Article 17)**:
  - Requires providers of high-risk AI systems to implement a **QMS** covering:
    - **Regulatory compliance strategy** (including conformity assessment).
    - **Techniques for design, design control, and design verification**.
    - **Development, quality control, and quality assurance procedures**.
    - **Examination, test, and validation procedures** (including post-market monitoring).
    - **Cybersecurity measures**.

**Integration**:
- The **MDR QMS** can be **expanded** to include **AI-specific processes** (e.g., data governance, model validation, cybersecurity).
- The **AI Act QMS** can **reference** the MDR’s existing QMS where applicable.

**Key Quote (AI Act, Article 17(1))**:
> *"Providers of high-risk AI systems shall put a quality management system in place that ensures compliance with this Regulation. That system shall be documented in a systematic and orderly manner in the form of written policies, procedures and instructions, and shall include at least the following aspects: [...]"*

**Key Quote (MDR, Article 10(9))**:
> *"Manufacturers shall establish, document, implement, maintain, keep up to date and continually improve a quality management system that shall ensure compliance with this Regulation in the most effective manner and in a manner that is proportionate to the risk class and the type of device."*

---

### **2. AI-Specific Gaps Not Covered by Standard MDR CE Marking**

While the MDR covers **general safety and performance requirements (GSPR)**, it does **not explicitly address AI-specific risks**. The AI Act introduces **additional obligations** that a hospital (as a **deployer** or **procurer**) should verify are **not fully covered by a standard MDR CE mark**:

#### **(a) Data Governance and Training Data Quality (AI Act, Article 10)**
- **MDR Gap**:
  - The MDR requires **clinical data** to demonstrate safety and performance (Annex XIV) but does **not specify requirements for training data** used in AI models.
  - The MDR does **not address bias, representativeness, or data quality** in AI training datasets.

- **AI Act Requirement**:
  - High-risk AI systems must be developed using **training, validation, and testing datasets** that meet **quality criteria** (Article 10(2)).
  - Datasets must be **relevant, representative, free of errors, and complete** (Article 10(3)).
  - **Bias mitigation measures** must be implemented (Article 10(5)).

**What a Hospital Should Check**:
- Does the **technical documentation** include:
  - **Data collection processes** (e.g., source of data, patient demographics, inclusion/exclusion criteria)?
  - **Data preprocessing steps** (e.g., cleaning, labelling, augmentation)?
  - **Bias assessment and mitigation measures** (e.g., fairness metrics, subgroup analysis)?
  - **Validation and testing protocols** (e.g., cross-validation, external validation)?

**Key Quote (AI Act, Article 10(2))**:
> *"Training, validation and testing data sets shall be subject to data governance and management practices appropriate for the intended purpose of the high-risk AI system. Those practices shall concern in particular: [...] the relevant design choices; data collection processes and the origin of data, and in the case of personal data, the original purpose of the data collection; relevant data-preparation processing operations, such as annotation, labelling, cleaning, updating, enrichment and aggregation; the formulation of assumptions, in particular with respect to the information that the data are supposed to measure and represent; [...]"*

---

#### **(b) Transparency and Explainability (AI Act, Article 13)**
- **MDR Gap**:
  - The MDR requires **instructions for use (IFU)** and **labelling** (Annex I, Chapter III) but does **not mandate explainability** for AI-driven decisions.
  - The MDR does **not require disclosure of AI model limitations** (e.g., confidence intervals, failure modes).

- **AI Act Requirement**:
  - High-risk AI systems must provide **clear and adequate information** to deployers (e.g., hospitals) on:
    - **Intended purpose** and **limitations** of the AI system.
    - **Performance characteristics** (e.g., accuracy, sensitivity, specificity).
    - **Known and foreseeable risks** (e.g., false positives/negatives, bias).
    - **Human oversight measures** (e.g., how clinicians can override AI decisions).
    - **Explainability** (e.g., how the AI system reaches its conclusions, where feasible).

**What a Hospital Should Check**:
- Does the **instructions for use (IFU)** include:
  - **Explainability measures** (e.g., feature importance, decision trees, confidence scores)?
  - **Limitations of the AI model** (e.g., patient subgroups where performance is lower)?
  - **Instructions for human oversight** (e.g., when to override AI recommendations)?
  - **Performance metrics** (e.g., sensitivity, specificity, positive predictive value) **stratified by patient subgroups**?

**Key Quote (AI Act, Article 13(1))**:
> *"High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed in such a way as to ensure that their operation is sufficiently transparent to enable deployers to interpret the system's output and use it appropriately. An appropriate type and degree of transparency shall be ensured, with a view to achieving compliance with the relevant obligations of the provider and the deployer set out in Chapter 3 of this Title."*

**Key Quote (AI Act, Article 13(3))**:
> *"Providers shall ensure that high-risk AI systems are accompanied by instructions for use in an appropriate digital format or otherwise that include concise, complete, correct and clear information that is relevant, accessible and comprehensible to deployers."*

---

#### **(c) Human Oversight (AI Act, Article 14)**
- **MDR Gap**:
  - The MDR requires **risk control measures** (Annex I, Section 4) but does **not explicitly mandate human oversight** for AI systems.
  - The MDR does **not define how clinicians should interact with AI systems** (e.g., override mechanisms, audit trails).

- **AI Act Requirement**:
  - High-risk AI systems must be designed to allow **effective human oversight** (Article 14).
  - **Human overseers** (e.g., clinicians) must be able to:
    - **Understand the AI system’s capabilities and limitations**.
    - **Monitor its operation** (e.g., real-time performance, drift detection).
    - **Override or reverse decisions** if necessary.
    - **Intervene in case of errors or failures**.

**What a Hospital Should Check**:
- Does the **AI system include**:
  - **Real-time monitoring tools** (e.g., alerts for model drift, performance degradation)?
  - **Override mechanisms** (e.g., clinician ability to reject AI recommendations)?
  - **Audit trails** (e.g., logging of AI decisions and clinician actions)?
  - **Training for clinicians** on how to use the AI system safely?

**Key Quote (AI Act, Article 14(1))**:
> *"High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed in such a way, including with appropriate human-machine interface tools, that they can be effectively overseen by natural persons during the period in which the AI system is in use."*

**Key Quote (AI Act, Article 14(4))**:
> *"Human oversight shall aim at preventing or minimising the risks to health, safety or fundamental rights that may emerge when a high-risk AI system is used in accordance with its intended purpose or under conditions of reasonably foreseeable misuse, in particular when such risks persist notwithstanding the application of other requirements set out in this Section."*

---

#### **(d) Accuracy, Robustness, and Cybersecurity (AI Act, Article 15)**
- **MDR Gap**:
  - The MDR requires **risk control measures** (Annex I, Section 4) but does **not explicitly address AI-specific robustness or cybersecurity risks**.
  - The MDR does **not mandate adversarial testing** (e.g., testing AI systems against malicious inputs).

- **AI Act Requirement**:
  - High-risk AI systems must achieve an **appropriate level of accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity** (Article 15).
  - **Robustness** includes:
    - **Resilience to errors, faults, and inconsistencies** (e.g., input perturbations, adversarial attacks).
    - **Fallback mechanisms** in case of failure.
  - **Cybersecurity** includes:
    - **Protection against hacking, data poisoning, and model inversion attacks**.
    - **Secure data transmission and storage**.

**What a Hospital Should Check**:
- Does the **technical documentation** include:
  - **Adversarial testing results** (e.g., robustness against input perturbations)?
  - **Cybersecurity risk assessment** (e.g., penetration testing, encryption standards)?
  - **Fallback mechanisms** (e.g., what happens if the AI system fails)?
  - **Data integrity measures** (e.g., protection against tampering)?

**Key Quote (AI Act, Article 15(1))**:
> *"High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed in such a way that they achieve an appropriate level of accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity, and that they perform consistently in those respects throughout their lifecycle."*

**Key Quote (AI Act, Article 15(4))**:
> *"High-risk AI systems shall be resilient as regards errors, faults or inconsistencies that may occur within the system or the environment in which the system operates, in particular due to their interaction with natural persons or other systems."*

---

#### **(e) Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (AI Act, Article 27)**
- **MDR Gap**:
  - The MDR focuses on **patient safety and clinical performance** but does **not explicitly address fundamental rights** (e.g., non-discrimination, privacy, autonomy).
  - The MDR does **not require an assessment of AI’s impact on equality or fairness**.

- **AI Act Requirement**:
  - Before placing a high-risk AI system on the market, the **provider must conduct a fundamental rights impact assessment (FRIA)** (Article 27).
  - The FRIA must assess **potential negative impacts on fundamental rights**, including:
    - **Non-discrimination** (e.g., bias against certain patient groups).
    - **Privacy and data protection** (e.g., GDPR compliance).
    - **Autonomy and informed consent** (e.g., patient understanding of AI-driven decisions).
    - **Access to healthcare** (e.g., whether the AI system disadvantages certain populations).

**What a Hospital Should Check**:
- Does the **technical documentation** include a **fundamental rights impact assessment (FRIA)**?
- Does the FRIA address:
  - **Bias and discrimination risks** (e.g., performance disparities across patient subgroups)?
  - **Privacy risks** (e.g., re-identification of anonymised data)?
  - **Patient autonomy** (e.g., transparency in AI-driven decisions)?
  - **Accessibility** (e.g., whether the AI system works for all patient groups)?

**Key Quote (AI Act, Article 27(1))**:
> *"Providers of high-risk AI systems shall perform an assessment of the impact on fundamental rights that the use of the system may produce. The assessment shall be carried out prior to the placing on the market or putting into service of the high-risk AI system."*

### **4. Practical Steps for Hospitals Procuring AI Medical Devices**
1. **Request AI-Specific Documentation**:
   - **Technical documentation** (Annex IV, AI Act) in addition to **MDR Annex II**.
   - **Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (FRIA)** (Article 27, AI Act).
   - **Post-market monitoring plan** (Article 72, AI Act).

2. **Verify AI-Specific Risk Controls**:
   - **Bias mitigation measures** (e.g., subgroup performance analysis).
   - **Explainability tools** (e.g., decision rationale, confidence scores).
   - **Human oversight mechanisms** (e.g., clinician override, audit logs).
   - **Cybersecurity measures** (e.g., encryption, adversarial testing).

3. **Assess Clinical Integration**:
   - **Training for clinicians** on AI system use and limitations.
   - **Workflow integration** (e.g., how AI recommendations are presented to clinicians).
   - **Fallback procedures** in case of AI failure.

4. **Monitor Post-Market Performance**:
   - **Track AI-specific incidents** (e.g., model drift, bias-related errors).
   - **Review post-market surveillance data** (e.g., real-world performance vs. clinical trials).
   - **Ensure compliance with AI Act reporting obligations** (e.g., serious incidents affecting fundamental rights).

---

### **5. Conclusion**
- The **AI Act and MDR are complementary**, with the AI Act filling **AI-specific gaps** not covered by the MDR.
- **Key AI-specific requirements** that hospitals should verify include:
  - **Data governance and bias mitigation** (Article 10).
  - **Transparency and explainability** (Article 13).
  - **Human oversight mechanisms** (Article 14).
  - **Accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity** (Article 15).
  - **Fundamental rights impact assessment** (Article 27).
- **Notified bodies** assessing medical devices with AI must **verify compliance with both MDR and AI Act requirements**.
- Hospitals should **request AI-specific documentation** and **assess clinical integration** to ensure safe and effective use of AI medical devices.
