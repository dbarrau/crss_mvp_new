You: Compare the obligations that the EU AI Act places on a deployer versus an importer of a high-risk AI system. Where do their responsibilities overlap and where do they diverge?

Agent: ### **1. Direct Answer: Obligations of Deployers vs. Importers of High-Risk AI Systems under the EU AI Act**
The **EU AI Act** distinguishes between the obligations of **deployers** and **importers** of high-risk AI systems, with some overlapping responsibilities but distinct primary focuses:

- **Deployers** (users of the AI system under their authority) are primarily responsible for **operational compliance**, including human oversight, monitoring, transparency, and incident reporting.
- **Importers** (entities placing high-risk AI systems from third countries on the EU market) are primarily responsible for **pre-market conformity verification**, traceability, and ensuring the system meets EU regulatory requirements before it reaches the market.

**Overlap**: Both must cooperate with authorities, monitor risks, and ensure compliance with the AI Act’s requirements.
**Divergence**: Importers focus on **pre-market checks** (e.g., CE marking, technical documentation), while deployers focus on **post-deployment use** (e.g., human oversight, logging, incident reporting).

### **2. Detailed Comparison with Regulatory References**

#### **A. Obligations of Importers (Article 23, EU AI Act)**
Importers must ensure the high-risk AI system complies with the AI Act **before placing it on the market** and maintain traceability. Key obligations include:

1. **Pre-Market Conformity Verification** (Article 23(1)):
   - Verify the provider has:
     - Completed the **conformity assessment procedure** (Article 43) [Article 23(1)(a)].
     - Drawn up **technical documentation** (Article 11, Annex IV) [Article 23(1)(b)].
     - Affixed the **CE marking** and provided the **EU declaration of conformity** (Article 47) [Article 23(1)(c)].
     - Appointed an **authorised representative** (Article 22(1)) [Article 23(1)(d)].
   - *"Before placing a high-risk AI system on the market, importers shall ensure that the system is in conformity with this Regulation by verifying that: [...] the relevant conformity assessment procedure referred to in Article 43 has been carried out by the provider of the high-risk AI system"* [Article 23(1)(a)].

2. **Non-Conformity and Risk Handling** (Article 23(2)):
   - If the system is non-compliant or presents a **risk** (as defined in Article 79(1)), the importer must:
     - **Not place it on the market** until corrected.
     - **Inform the provider, authorised representative, and market surveillance authorities** if a risk exists.
   - *"Where an importer has sufficient reason to consider that a high-risk AI system is not in conformity with this Regulation, or is falsified, or accompanied by falsified documentation, it shall not place the system on the market until it has been brought into conformity. Where the high-risk AI system presents a risk within the meaning of Article 79(1), the importer shall inform the provider of the system, the authorised representative and the market surveillance authorities to that effect"* [Article 23(2)].

3. **Traceability and Identification** (Article 23(3)):
   - Importers must **label the system** with their name, trade name/trademark, and contact address.
   - *"Importers shall indicate their name, registered trade name or registered trade mark, and the address at which they can be contacted on the high-risk AI system and on its packaging or its accompanying documentation, where applicable"* [Article 23(3)].

4. **Post-Market Responsibilities** (Article 23(4)-(7)):
   - Ensure **storage/transport conditions** do not jeopardise compliance (Article 23(4)).
   - **Retain documentation** (e.g., EU declaration of conformity, instructions for use) for **10 years** (Article 23(5)).
   - **Cooperate with authorities** and provide documentation upon request (Article 23(6)-(7)).

---

#### **B. Obligations of Deployers (Article 26, EU AI Act)**
Deployers (users of the AI system under their authority) must ensure **safe and compliant use** of the system during operation. Key obligations include:

1. **Human Oversight** (Article 26(1)):
   - Ensure the system is **overseen by natural persons** with the necessary competence, training, and authority (as required by Article 14).
   - *"Deployers of high-risk AI systems shall ensure that the systems are used in accordance with the instructions of use accompanying the systems, pursuant to Article 13(2)"* [Article 26(1)].

2. **Monitoring and Incident Reporting** (Article 26(2)-(3)):
   - **Monitor the system’s operation** based on instructions for use.
   - **Report serious incidents or malfunctions** to the provider or distributor (and, where applicable, the importer).
   - *"Deployers shall keep the logs automatically generated by the high-risk AI system, to the extent such logs are under their control, for a period ending six months after the high-risk AI system has ceased to be used"* [Article 26(2)].
   - *"Deployers shall, without undue delay, inform the provider or distributor and, where applicable, the importer and the relevant market surveillance authority of any serious incident or any malfunctioning of those systems which constitutes a breach of obligations under Union law intended to protect fundamental rights"* [Article 26(3)].

3. **Transparency and Record-Keeping** (Article 26(4)-(5)):
   - **Inform affected persons** when they are interacting with an AI system (unless obvious).
   - **Keep logs** for **6 months** after the system ceases to be used.
   - *"Deployers of high-risk AI systems that are subject to requirements regarding the logging of data under Union or national law shall retain the logs automatically generated by that high-risk AI system, to the extent such logs are under their control"* [Article 26(4)].

4. **Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (FRIA)** (Article 27):
   - For **public sector deployers**, conduct a **FRIA** before using the system (unless already performed by the provider).
   - *"Before putting into service or using a high-risk AI system referred to in Annex III, deployers that are public authorities, agencies or bodies, or natural or legal persons acting on their behalf, shall perform an assessment of the impact on fundamental rights that the use of such system may produce"* [Article 27(1)].

### **3. Overlap and Divergence**

| **Aspect**               | **Importer (Article 23)**                          | **Deployer (Article 26)**                          | **Overlap/Divergence**                          |
|--------------------------|----------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| **Pre-Market Checks**    | ✅ Verify conformity (CE marking, documentation).  | ❌ Not applicable.                                 | **Diverge**: Importers focus on pre-market.    |
| **Traceability**         | ✅ Label system with importer details.             | ❌ Not required.                                   | **Diverge**: Importers ensure traceability.    |
| **Risk Reporting**       | ✅ Report risks to authorities (Article 79(1)).    | ✅ Report serious incidents/malfunctions.           | **Overlap**: Both must report risks.           |
| **Documentation Retention** | ✅ Keep EU declaration, instructions for **10 years**. | ✅ Keep logs for **6 months**.                     | **Diverge**: Different retention periods.      |
| **Human Oversight**      | ❌ Not required.                                   | ✅ Ensure competent human oversight.                | **Diverge**: Deployers manage operational use. |
| **Monitoring**           | ✅ Ensure storage/transport compliance.            | ✅ Monitor system operation.                       | **Overlap**: Both monitor, but for different purposes. |
| **Cooperation with Authorities** | ✅ Provide documentation upon request.       | ✅ Cooperate in investigations.                    | **Overlap**: Both must assist authorities.     |
| **Fundamental Rights Impact** | ❌ Not required.                          | ✅ Conduct FRIA (public sector only).               | **Diverge**: Deployers assess impact.          |

---

### **4. Key Inferences**
1. **Importers act as a "gatekeeper"** for high-risk AI systems entering the EU market, ensuring they meet regulatory standards before deployment.
2. **Deployers act as "operators"**, ensuring the system is used safely and in compliance with instructions, with a focus on **post-deployment monitoring**.
3. **Both must report risks**, but importers focus on **pre-market risks**, while deployers report **operational incidents**.
4. **Documentation retention periods differ**: Importers keep records for **10 years** (pre-market focus), while deployers keep logs for **6 months** (operational focus).
