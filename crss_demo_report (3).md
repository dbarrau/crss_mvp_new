## Question 1

What are all obligations of providers operating High Risk AI systems and GPAI, according to the EU AI Act?

### Answer

### **Obligations of Providers under the EU AI Act for High-Risk AI Systems and General-Purpose AI (GPAI) Models**

The **EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689)** imposes distinct obligations on **providers** of **High-Risk AI Systems** and **General-Purpose AI (GPAI) Models**. Below is a structured breakdown of these obligations, grounded in the **REGULATORY CONTEXT** provided.

---

## **I. Obligations for Providers of High-Risk AI Systems**
*(Title III, Chapter 2 of the AI Act)*

### **1. Risk Management System (Article 9)**
Providers must establish, implement, document, and maintain a **risk management system** throughout the AI system’s lifecycle.

> **Verbatim Quote:**
> *"A risk management system shall be established, implemented, documented and maintained in relation to high-risk AI systems."* **[1] Article 9(1)**

**Key Requirements:**
- **Continuous iterative process** (Article 9(2)) – ongoing identification, estimation, and evaluation of risks.
- **Mitigation measures** must be implemented and tested (Article 9(4)).
- **Residual risks** must be communicated to deployers (Article 9(7)).

**Cross-reference:**
- Recital 61 emphasizes that risk management must be **proportionate** to the risks posed.

---

### **2. Data and Data Governance (Article 10)**
High-risk AI systems must be trained, validated, and tested using **high-quality datasets**.

> **Verbatim Quote:**
> *"Training, validation and testing data sets shall be subject to appropriate data governance and management practices."* **[1] Article 10(2)**

**Key Requirements:**
- **Relevance, representativeness, and absence of errors/bias** (Article 10(2)(a)-(f)).
- **Data governance measures** (e.g., data collection processes, bias detection, data gaps analysis).
- **Documentation of data sources and characteristics** (Article 10(3)).

**Cross-reference:**
- Recital 63 clarifies that **bias mitigation** is a key objective.

---

### **3. Technical Documentation (Article 11)**
Providers must draw up **technical documentation** before placing a high-risk AI system on the market.

> **Verbatim Quote:**
> *"Providers shall draw up the technical documentation of the high-risk AI system in accordance with Annex IV."* **[1] Article 11(1)**

**Key Requirements:**
- Must include **Annex IV** details (e.g., system description, risk management, monitoring, conformity assessment).
- Must be kept up to date (Article 11(2)).
- Must be made available to **national authorities** upon request (Article 11(3)).

**Cross-reference:**
- Annex IV lists **20+ mandatory documentation elements**.

---

### **4. Record-Keeping (Automated Logging) (Article 12)**
High-risk AI systems must **automatically record events** (logs) to ensure traceability.

> **Verbatim Quote:**
> *"High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed with capabilities enabling the automatic recording of events (‘logs’) over the duration of the lifetime of the system."* **[1] Article 12(1)**

**Key Requirements:**
- Logs must be **sufficiently detailed** to monitor compliance (Article 12(2)).
- Must be retained for **at least 6 months** (unless other EU laws require longer retention) (Article 12(3)).

**Cross-reference:**
- Recital 65 states that logs must enable **post-market monitoring**.

---

### **5. Transparency and Provision of Information to Deployers (Article 13)**
Providers must ensure high-risk AI systems are **transparent** and accompanied by **instructions for use**.

> **Verbatim Quote:**
> *"High-risk AI systems shall be accompanied by instructions for use in an appropriate digital format or otherwise that include concise, complete, correct and clear information that is relevant, accessible and comprehensible to the deployers."* **[1] Article 13(1)**

**Key Requirements:**
- **Instructions must include:**
  - System’s **intended purpose** (Article 13(1)(a)).
  - **Performance characteristics** (Article 13(1)(b)).
  - **Risks and mitigation measures** (Article 13(1)(c)).
  - **Human oversight measures** (Article 13(1)(d)).
  - **Expected lifetime and maintenance requirements** (Article 13(1)(e)).

**Cross-reference:**
- Recital 66 emphasizes **user-friendly** information.

---

### **6. Human Oversight (Article 14)**
High-risk AI systems must be designed to enable **effective human oversight**.

> **Verbatim Quote:**
> *"High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed in such a way, including with appropriate human-machine interface tools, that they can be effectively overseen by natural persons during the period in which the AI system is in use."* **[1] Article 14(1)**

**Key Requirements:**
- Must allow **human intervention** (e.g., override, shutdown) (Article 14(3)).
- Must provide **clear information** to the human overseer (Article 14(4)).

**Cross-reference:**
- Recital 67 states that oversight should **prevent or minimize risks**.

---

### **7. Accuracy, Robustness, and Cybersecurity (Article 15)**
High-risk AI systems must achieve **appropriate levels of accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity**.

> **Verbatim Quote:**
> *"High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed in such a way that they achieve, in the light of their intended purpose, an appropriate level of accuracy, robustness and cybersecurity, and perform consistently in those respects throughout their lifecycle."* **[1] Article 15(1)**

**Key Requirements:**
- **Accuracy metrics** must be disclosed (Article 15(2)).
- **Resilience against errors, faults, and inconsistencies** (Article 15(3)).
- **Cybersecurity protections** against adversarial attacks (Article 15(4)).

**Cross-reference:**
- Recital 68 links robustness to **adversarial testing**.

---

### **8. Conformity Assessment (Article 43)**
Before placing a high-risk AI system on the market, providers must undergo a **conformity assessment**.

> **Verbatim Quote:**
> *"Before placing a high-risk AI system on the market or putting it into service, the provider shall ensure that the system has undergone the relevant conformity assessment procedure in accordance with Article 43."* **[1] Article 16(a)**

**Key Requirements:**
- **Internal control (Annex VI)** for most high-risk AI systems (Article 43(2)).
- **Third-party conformity assessment (Annex VII)** for certain systems (e.g., biometric identification) (Article 43(3)).
- **EU declaration of conformity** must be drawn up (Article 47).

**Cross-reference:**
- Article 48 requires **CE marking** after successful assessment.

---

### **9. Post-Market Monitoring (Article 72)**
Providers must establish a **post-market monitoring system** to track real-world performance.

> **Verbatim Quote:**
> *"Providers of high-risk AI systems shall establish and document a post-market monitoring system in a manner that is proportionate to the nature of the artificial intelligence technologies and the risks of the high-risk AI system."* **[1] Article 72(1)**

**Key Requirements:**
- **Continuous collection and review** of data (Article 72(2)).
- **Reporting of serious incidents** to national authorities (Article 73).
- **Corrective actions** if risks emerge (Article 74).

**Cross-reference:**
- Recital 104 emphasizes **proactive monitoring**.

---

### **10. Registration in EU Database (Article 49)**
Providers must register high-risk AI systems in the **EU database** before market placement.

> **Verbatim Quote:**
> *"Providers of high-risk AI systems shall, prior to placing them on the market or putting them into service, register themselves and their high-risk AI systems in the EU database referred to in Article 71."* **[1] Article 49(1)**

**Key Requirements:**
- **Mandatory registration** for all high-risk AI systems (except those under Annex III, point 5(b)).
- **Updates** if system modifications occur (Article 49(3)).

## **II. Obligations for Providers of General-Purpose AI (GPAI) Models**
*(Title IV, Chapter 5 of the AI Act)*

### **1. Transparency Obligations (Article 52)**
Providers of GPAI models must **document and disclose** certain information.

> **Verbatim Quote:**
> *"Providers of general-purpose AI models shall draw up and make available technical documentation to any provider of AI systems that intends to integrate the general-purpose AI model into its AI system."* **[1] Article 52(1)(a)**

**Key Requirements:**
- **Technical documentation** (training data, model architecture, performance metrics).
- **Summary of training data** (Article 52(1)(c)).
- **Compliance with copyright law** (Article 52(1)(d)).

**Cross-reference:**
- Recital 105 states that transparency enables **downstream compliance**.

---

### **2. Cooperation with Downstream Providers (Article 52(2))**
GPAI providers must **support compliance** of AI system providers integrating their models.

> **Verbatim Quote:**
> *"Providers of general-purpose AI models shall, upon request by a provider of an AI system that integrates the general-purpose AI model into its AI system, provide that provider with the necessary information and, where applicable, the technical access to enable the provider of the AI system to comply with the obligations laid down in this Regulation."* **[1] Article 52(2)**

**Key Requirements:**
- **Information sharing** on model capabilities and limitations.
- **Technical access** (e.g., APIs) if needed for compliance.

---

### **3. Systemic Risk Management (Article 55 – for GPAI with Systemic Risk)**
GPAI models deemed to pose a **systemic risk** (e.g., very high computational power) have **additional obligations**.

> **Verbatim Quote:**
> *"Providers of general-purpose AI models that are considered to pose a systemic risk shall perform model evaluation, assess and mitigate systemic risks, keep track of, document and report without undue delay to the Commission and the national competent authorities serious incidents and possible corrective measures to address them."* **[1] Article 55(1)**

**Key Requirements:**
- **Model evaluations** (including adversarial testing).
- **Risk mitigation measures**.
- **Incident reporting** to authorities.

**Cross-reference:**
- **Systemic risk** is defined in **Article 3(65)** (not provided in context, but referenced in Article 55).

---

## **III. Overlapping Obligations (High-Risk AI + GPAI)**
If a **GPAI model is integrated into a high-risk AI system**, the provider must comply with **both sets of obligations**:
- **GPAI transparency** (Article 52) → **High-risk risk management** (Article 9).
- **GPAI documentation** → **High-risk technical documentation** (Article 11).
- **GPAI systemic risk** (if applicable) → **High-risk conformity assessment** (Article 43).

> **Verbatim Quote (on overlap):**
> *"Where a general-purpose AI model is integrated into a high-risk AI system, the provider of that system shall comply with the obligations for high-risk AI systems set out in Chapter 2 of this Title."* **[1] Article 52(3)**

## **V. Legal Hierarchy & Cross-Regulation Considerations**
- **MDR/IVDR Overlap:** If a high-risk AI system is also a **medical device**, **MDR/IVDR conformity assessment** applies **instead of** the AI Act’s conformity assessment (Article 43(3) AI Act).
- **Binding vs. Guidance:** The AI Act is **binding law**, while **MDCG guidance** (e.g., on clinical evaluation) does not override it.
