## Query 1: Under the EU AI Act, when a high-risk AI system is also a medical device under MDR, which conformity assessment procesdure applies and what are the specific documentation requirements from Annex IV?

Agent: 1. Direct answer:
When a high-risk AI system is also a medical device under the MDR, the conformity assessment procedure under the EU AI Act must be followed in addition to the MDR conformity assessment. The technical documentation must comply with both the EU AI Act (Annex IV) and the MDR (Annex II and III), and a single EU declaration of conformity must cover both sets of requirements.

---

2. Relevant quotes/paraphrases:

- **EU AI Act conformity assessment applicability**:
  [2] Subparagraph 3 of Article 43: *"For high-risk AI systems covered by the Union harmonisation legislation listed in Section A of Annex I, the provider shall follow the relevant conformity assessment procedure as required under those legal acts. The requirements set out in Section 2 of this Chapter shall apply to those high-risk AI systems and shall be part of that assessment. Points 4.3., 4.4., 4.5. and the fifth paragraph of point 4.7 of Annex VII shall apply mutatis mutandis."*

- **EU AI Act technical documentation requirements (Annex IV)**:
  [5] Subparagraph 2 of Article 11: *"The technical documentation shall be drawn up in such a way as to demonstrate that the high-risk AI system complies with the requirements set out in this Section and to provide national competent authorities and notified bodies with the necessary information in a clear and comprehensive form to assess the compliance of the AI system with those requirements. It shall contain, at a minimum, the elements set out in Annex IV."*

  [5] Paragraph 2 of Article 11: *"Where a high-risk AI system related to a product covered by the Union harmonisation legislation listed in Section A of Annex I is placed on the market or put into service, a single set of technical documentation shall be drawn up containing all the information set out in paragraph 1, as well as the information required under those legal acts."*

  [3] Paragraph 3 of Article 47: *"Where high-risk AI systems are subject to other Union harmonisation legislation which also requires an EU declaration of conformity, a single EU declaration of conformity shall be drawn up in respect of all Union law applicable to the high-risk AI system."*

- the placing on the market, the putting into service or the use of an AI system that deploys subliminal techniques beyond a person’s consciousness or purposefully manipulative or deceptive techniques, with the objective, or the effect of materially distorting the behaviour of a person or a group of persons by appreciably impairing their ability to make an informed decision, thereby causing them to take a decision that they would not have otherwise taken in a manner that causes or is reasonably likely to cause that person, another person or group of persons significant harm;
- the placing on the market, the putting into service or the use of an AI system that exploits any of the vulnerabilities of a natural person or a specific group of persons due to their age, disability or a specific social or economic situation, with the objective, or the effect, of materially distorting the behaviour of that person or a person belonging to that group in a manner that causes or is reasonably likely to cause that person or another person significant harm;

## Query 3: Compare the obligations that the EU AI Act places on a deployer versus an importer of a high-risk ay system. Where do their responsibilities overlap and where do they diverge? 

Agent: ### Direct Answer:
The EU AI Act imposes distinct but overlapping obligations on **deployers** and **importers** of high-risk AI systems. Both roles must ensure compliance with the Act, but their responsibilities differ in scope, focus, and enforcement mechanisms. Below is a structured comparison of their obligations, highlighting overlaps and divergences.

---

### **Obligations of Deployers (Article 26)**
Deployers are primarily responsible for the **use** of high-risk AI systems in accordance with the Act and the provider’s instructions. Key obligations include:

1. **General Use Obligations**:
   - Deployers must use high-risk AI systems in line with the instructions for use (Article 26(1)).
   - They must assign human oversight to competent personnel with adequate training and authority (Article 26(2)).
   - They must monitor the operation of the system and inform providers of any issues (Article 26(5)).

2. **Data and Transparency Obligations**:
   - If the deployer controls input data, they must ensure it is relevant and representative for the system’s intended purpose (Article 26(4)).
   - Deployers must inform affected individuals when they are subject to the use of high-risk AI systems (Article 26(11)).
   - They must cooperate with competent authorities and provide requested information (Article 26(12)).

3. **Special Cases**:
   - Public authorities or EU institutions must register high-risk AI systems in the EU database and cannot use unregistered systems (Article 26(8)).
   - Employers must inform workers’ representatives and affected workers before using high-risk AI systems in the workplace (Article 26(7)).
   - Deployers must conduct data protection impact assessments where required (Article 26(9)).

4. **Logging and Reporting**:
   - Deployers must retain logs generated by the system if under their control (Article 26(6)).
   - For post-remote biometric identification systems, deployers must submit annual reports to authorities (Article 26, Subparagraph 6).

---

### **Obligations of Importers (Article 23)**
Importers are responsible for ensuring that high-risk AI systems placed on the EU market comply with the Act **before** they are made available. Key obligations include:

1. **Pre-Market Compliance Verification**:
   - Importers must verify that the system has undergone the required conformity assessment (Article 23(1)(a)).
   - They must confirm that the provider has drawn up technical documentation (Article 23(1)(b)) and that the system bears the CE marking and is accompanied by the EU declaration of conformity and instructions for use (Article 23(1)(c), Point (c)).
   - They must ensure the provider has appointed an authorized representative (Article 23(1)(d), Point (d)).

2. **Post-Market Compliance**:
   - If an importer suspects non-compliance or falsification, they must not place the system on the market until it is corrected (Article 23(2)).
   - They must ensure storage and transport conditions do not jeopardize compliance (Article 23(4)).
   - They must label the system with their name, trade name, or trademark (Article 23(3)).

3. **Documentation and Cooperation**:
   - Importers must retain copies of certificates, instructions, and EU declarations of conformity for 10 years (Article 23(5)).
   - They must provide competent authorities with requested information to demonstrate compliance (Article 23(6)).
   - They must cooperate with authorities to mitigate risks (Article 23(7)).

---

### **Overlaps Between Deployers and Importers**
1. **Cooperation with Authorities**:
   - Both deployers (Article 26(12)) and importers (Article 23(7)) must cooperate with competent authorities in investigations or risk mitigation.

2. **Non-Compliance Handling**:
   - Both must refrain from placing or using non-compliant systems until issues are resolved (deployers: Article 26(1); importers: Article 23(2)).

3. **Documentation Retention**:
   - Both must retain relevant documentation (deployers: logs under their control, Article 26(6); importers: certificates and declarations, Article 23(5)).

---

### **Divergences Between Deployers and Importers**
| **Aspect**               | **Deployers**                                                                 | **Importers**                                                                 |
